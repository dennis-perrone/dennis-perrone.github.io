# What is Risk Management Framework (RMF)
Risk Management Framework or RMF is the system that the United States federal government utilizes to ensure a cyber security and risk-based compliance throughout the lifecycle of an Information System. It is the byproduct of a few factors. Initially, there was the E-Government Act ([Public Law 107-347](https://www.congress.gov/107/plaws/publ347/PLAW-107publ347.pdf)) which led to the [Federal Information Security Modernization Act](https://www.congress.gov/113/plaws/publ283/PLAW-113publ283.pdf) or FISMA. 

According to NIST, the main purpose of RMF is to manage organizational risk. 

This is accomplished by executing a 6 step process. These processes will be covered in more depth in a future post. For the sake of exposure, those steps are:
1. Categorize
2. Select
3. Implement
4. Assess
5. Authorize
6. Monitor
