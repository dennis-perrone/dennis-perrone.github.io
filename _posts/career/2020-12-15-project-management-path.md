## Overview

This post will serve as a landing page of sorts for my project management study and progression. That means that I'll probably be updating it from time to time. This is meant to be a general introduction and references for moving forward.

## Certifications

If I go this route, I'll eventually want to earn my Project Management Professional (PMP) certificate. I hold multiple IT certifications and understand that on the surface, the letters matter. In reality, the value of certifications come from the knowledge gained and the ability to apply that knowledge to current and future roles. I'm also very adamant on the idea of training your replacement. I remember hearing a few times in my past that if you can't be replaced, you can be promoted. Plus, mentoring provides an awesome medium to contribute back and pay forward the assistance and experiences you've gotten along your own journey.

In the past, my certification studying has felt fairly haphazard and I'm not a fan of that. I have found that it was hard for me to lay a road map and ensuring that I covered all topics. Not only that, but by not having a defined scope it really led to an unfocused study. Considering the scope and purpose of this exam, I want to treat it like a project and take a very structured approach to achieving those 3 letters.

## My Reasoning

I've been fortunate to serve under and alongside of some fantastic managers. With that, I've also dealt with my share of less than stellar ones. Taking the good and the bad, I'm able to apply the different management styles when I'm put in any type of leadership role. I'd love to be able to grow as a manager and apply those principles along with getting out of my comfort zone. In addition to leading and mentoring, there is multiple facets of project and program management such as finance, risk, and planning that I find interesting.

## Anticipated Future Posts

There are a few paths that I would intend to travel down. When I begin my study for PMP, I'll break down topics and subtopics from the blueprint and the PMBOK study guide. Additionally, I plan on making sure that I fully understand every topic.
